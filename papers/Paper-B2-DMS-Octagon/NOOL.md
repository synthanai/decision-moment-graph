# நூல் / NOOL — DMS Octagon Paper (B2)

---

## நோக்கம் / Intent (Soul: WHY)

### The Problem
1. **Decision governance is episodic, not continuous.** Boards review quarterly. Strategy sessions happen annually. But decisions happen continuously. No standard treats the decision moment as the atomic unit of organizational life.
2. **AI decisions lack governance gates.** Agentic AI scales decisions without proportional governance. RAMP calibration (risk-adjusted scrutiny) is missing.
3. **Existing decision models are incomplete.** OODA (4 phases, no governance), PDCA (4 phases, no deliberation), Design Thinking (5 phases, no auditability). None include dissent, reversibility, or retrospective learning.

### The Purpose
A methodology paper that formalizes the 8-phase DMS Octagon as a decision governance standard, positions it against OODA/PDCA/Design Thinking, introduces RAMP risk calibration, and demonstrates with worked examples.

### What We're Avoiding
| Anti-Pattern | How We Prevent It |
|--------------|-------------------|
| "Just another framework" | Show it subsumes OODA/PDCA as degenerate cases |
| Overclaiming AI-specific | Present as universal (human + AI), with AI as amplifier |
| Full SYNTHAI vocabulary | Only DMS, Octagon, RAMP, MERIT. No kits, no MBS |

---

## வடிவம் / Abstraction (Mind: WHAT TYPE)
**DESIGN**: Formalize a decision governance protocol with formal notation and evaluation.

### The 8 Phases
| Phase | Function | Gate |
|-------|----------|------|
| FRAME | Situation recognition, context setting | Is the question well-formed? |
| SPAR | Multi-agent dialectic deliberation | Were tensions surfaced? |
| GATE | Risk-adjusted approval (RAMP) | Is scrutiny proportional to risk? |
| COMMIT | Binding decision record | Is the commitment auditable? |
| ENACT | Execution and tool-use | Were actions reversible? |
| YIELD | Production delivery | Did the output match the commitment? |
| GAUGE | Outcome assessment | What actually happened? |
| RETRO | Process reflection and learning | What would we do differently? |
